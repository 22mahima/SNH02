Success Stories

Story 1: Deepika Padukone

It was the first time when an A-lister actually chose to come out in the open to declare her mental status to everyone. She addressed to the entire world and talked it all out about how she suffered from serious depression because of her failed relationship and how the entire illness took a toll on her. Deepika was at the peak of her career and very well knew that this could backlash on her profession, but she cared little and gave out ways to cope for people who go through the same kind of illnesses. by Pratibha Devasenapathy.

Story 2:Robin Uthappa

One notable Indian sportsman who openly discussed his battle with depression and managed to overcome it is cricketer Robin Uthappa. Robin Uthappa is a talented Indian cricketer who has represented the Indian national team in both One Day Internationals (ODIs) and T20 matches. He has also been a key player in the Indian Premier League (IPL). In 2017, Robin Uthappa opened up about his struggle with clinical depression during the early years of his cricketing career. He spoke about the intense pressure, expectations, and the emotional toll that professional cricket can take on players. Uthappa shared his experiences to raise awareness about mental health issues in sports and to encourage others to seek help. Overcoming the challenges of depression, Uthappa continued to play cricket and remained a valuable asset to his teams. His openness about mental health issues contributed to the growing conversation about mental well-being in Indian sports. It's important to note that mental health challenges can affect individuals in any profession, including elite athletes, and seeking support is a crucial step towards recovery..

Story 3:Vishal [Tamil actor]

Prominent faces in the Tamil film industry on Thursday came forward to talk about their experiences with depression and how they overcame it. Some in the industry had lost a friend to depression, others a family member, many in the prime of their lives without even realising it. “I woke up one morning feeling I had fallen and I was struggling to get up I felt alone,” saidActor Vishal. He said he was however fortunate enough to be able to draw strength from within. “I would look at my reflection and imagine I was talking to my conscience. I said I could do it,” said the actor at a programme held to announce the World Health Day Summit, scheduled to be held in New Delhi on April 6 and 7. Vishal said there were many in the industry who suffered from depression but were too afraid to talk fearing it would upset their careers.

Story Boards

Story 1: The Garden of Resilience

Once upon a time in a small village, there lived a woman named Maya. Maya had faced many challenges in life, and at times, it felt like the weight of the world was on her shoulders. One day, as she walked through the village, she stumbled upon an abandoned garden. Despite its neglect, Maya saw potential in the garden. She decided to nurture it back to life. Day by day, she tended to the soil, planted new seeds, and watered the plants. Slowly, the garden began to flourish. As the flowers bloomed and the trees regained their vitality, Maya found a sense of purpose and joy. The act of cultivating life in the garden mirrored her own journey of resilience. The vibrant colors and fragrant blossoms became a daily reminder that, just like the garden, she too could grow through adversity. Word spread about Maya's magical garden, and soon the whole village came to enjoy its beauty. The garden became a symbol of hope and resilience, teaching everyone that even in the midst of neglect, life could be revived with care and determination..

Story 2: The Tale of Gratitude

In a quaint town, there lived a man named Sam who, despite facing hardships, found solace in a simple practice—gratitude. Every evening, Sam would sit by the window and reflect on three things he was grateful for that day. One day, as he faced particularly challenging times, Sam found it hard to identify positive aspects. Instead of giving up, he decided to express gratitude for the small things—a warm cup of tea, the comforting sound of rain, and the support of a friend. As days turned into weeks, Sam's gratitude practice transformed his perspective. He began to notice the beauty in ordinary moments and appreciate the kindness of others. Slowly, his mindset shifted from focusing on what went wrong to acknowledging the many things that went right. One evening, as Sam looked out of his window, he saw a rainbow after a refreshing rain. It dawned on him that even after the stormiest days, there could be a burst of color and light. The rainbow became a symbol of resilience and the beauty that follows adversity. Sam's tale spread through the town, inspiring others to adopt a gratitude mindset. The simple act of appreciating life's small blessings became a powerful tool for combating despair and finding joy in the present moment..

Book Recommendations

Lost Connections

by Johann Hari

Connections explains why depression affects so many people and that improving our relationships, not taking medication, is the way to beat our mental health problems.

Furiously Happy

by Jenny Lawson

In Furiously Happy, a humor memoir tinged with just enough tragedy and pathos to make it worthwhile, Jenny Lawson examines her own experience with severe depression and a host of other conditions, and explains how it has led her to live life to the fullest.
